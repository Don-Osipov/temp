Opportunities for VCs

Though there are abundant curricula in entrepreneurship, the students say the courses are not too helpful in actually creating a company. They would much rather go to the university’s innovation spaces instead. Moreover, the Technology Entrepreneur Center is beneficial, but there are lots of hyperboles for what they claim to do and what they actually do. You basically just meet founders who give talks. Consensus on accelerators on campus is that they put you in a room with investors, and you don’t get a lot done. Sadly, they also take most of the equity.

A partnership with Forge and iVentures would be most beneficial. The student-run organization has eyes and ears all across campus and runs small-scale competitions to build an early relationship with any potential founders. iVentures aggregate teams who are serious enough about their ventures to commit an entire summer. This accelerator will also naturally provide an avenue into the administration’s network. Since competition with other funds is still relatively local, now is an opportune time to scout UIUC, a school with an underrated track record in startups and venture capital.

2.

Cozad is an early-stage startup pitch competition that happens over two months.

3.
Forge

Forge is the largest student-run startup weekend, where teams pitch and can win up to $1,500.
